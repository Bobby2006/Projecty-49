For the initial 1500 years or so of its existence, origami was actually known as orikata, a Japanese word that translates to “folded paper”.

Paper was originally created sometime around 100 A.D. While paper is considered a common and cheap commodity now, at the time of its conception, the resources required to create paper were not as easily available.

Origami Butterflies and other simple designs are often given along with gifts and paper wrapping, at most Wedding Rituals but most the important is the presentation of 1000 paper cranes.

The first book ever published about origami was entitled “Sembazuru Orikata” (Thousand Crane Folding), written by Akisato Rito, which was about the timeless custom of folding a 1000 paper cranes for traditional weddings and the wishes it could grant.

As a good luck token for all the future dangers to be faced, samurais commonly used to present gifts of origami to each other. These gifts were usually a type of ceremonial origami, folded from multiple pieces of paper into intricate shapes and presented along with a strip of dried fish or meat, to signify “good wishes”.

Interesting Facts About Cranes

  • There are 15 species of crane in 4 genera.
  • Cranes are found on all continents except in South America and Antarctica. They live in wetlands and grasslands habitats.
  • The lifespan is from 20 to 50 years in the wild, depending on the species. Oldest known crane was the Siberian Crane that lived 83 years in captivity.

Interesting Facts About Dragons

  1. The name dragon is derived from the Latin word 'draconem' which means 'huge serpent'.
  2. Western or European dragons tend to be very large, fire-breathing, serpent-like, scaly creatures with wings, while Eastern or Chinese dragons tend to be smaller creatures, with no wings, horns for ears and are usually friends of humans.
  3. The Ancient Celtics believed that dragons were capable of prophesizing and they worshipped them.
